Bookshelf

I have received requests for book recommendations by my friends and family and hence decided to create a sort of virtual bookshelf. I have benefited a lot from people who share their books online and I hope this will benefit you too. What consists below is a diverse range of books consisting of history, finance, psychology, science and so on. Hope you enjoy.

I have probably read about 95% of these books and have learnt a lot from each of them. If one can, conservatively speaking, acquire an idea or two per book, one would have 1000-2000 ideas after a thousand books. How many people go through life acquiring a thousand new ideas?

Here are some quotes by eminent people whom I admire. If you need some reminder on why you should pickup a damn book, feel free to go over these quotes.

In my whole life, I have known no wise people (over a broad subject matter area) who didn't read all the time—none. Zero. - Charlie Munger

Read 500 pages like this every day. That's how knowledge works. It builds up, like compound interest. All of you can do it, but I guarantee not many of you will do it. -Warren Buffett

At one point, I ran out of books to read at the school library and the neighborhood library. This is maybe the third or fourth grade. I tried to convince the librarian to order books for me. So then, I started to read the Encyclopedia Brittanica. That was so helpful. You don’t know what you don’t know. You realize there are all these things out there. -Elon Musk
